# Final Project Idea

A living digital map where people can preserve the meaning of places before they disappear, change, or are forgotten.

The project is about saving memories tied to real locations: stories from people, old and new photographs, local traditions, words from languages or dialects, food memories, festivals, buildings, rivers, forests, streets, and signs of environmental or cultural change.

The main idea is that normal maps show where a place is, but they do not show what the place meant to the people who lived there. This project would let communities record those meanings so future generations can understand not just the location, but the life around it.

It matters because climate change, urbanization, migration, and globalization are making many places and traditions disappear. If people do not record them now, many memories may vanish permanently.

The first version can focus on one city, neighborhood, school, camp, or local area instead of trying to cover the whole world immediately.
