Vaquita memory record

Phocoena sinus

The rarest marine mammal on Earth

The vaquita was first identified in 1958. In a little more than half a century, it has been pushed to the edge of extinction by accidental capture in fishing nets.

Status Critically endangered
Population About 10 individuals
Habitat Northern Gulf of California
Size Up to 5 feet, 120 pounds

Reason for extinction risk

The danger is not hunting. It is entanglement.

Vaquitas drown after becoming trapped in gillnets set for other marine animals, especially totoaba. Illegal totoaba fishing grew because of demand for swim bladders, and that trade now drives the vaquita's collapse.

Extinction pressure

How a species becomes a whisper

1958

The vaquita is formally discovered by science.

1970s

Gillnet entanglement pushes the species down to dangerously low levels.

2014

WWF calls for strong enforcement of a gillnet ban across the full vaquita range.

Now

Only around 10 are estimated to remain, so every surviving animal matters.
